Dangers


ISP Considerations

Be careful when testing a public IP address, as ISPs may be unhappy if you enumerate public IPs at high speeds. This isn't limited to port scans, & applies to any action that generates a lot of potentially "suspicious" traffic.

Some ISPs may be more restrictive than others, & it can be hard to predict what actions they may flag as potentially maliclous. An ISPs standards may even change over time.

As a worst case scenario, I have heard of a Bug Bounty hunter that got dropped by their ISP who was concerned about them hacking websites. They resorted to using rented Cloud Servers for their Bug Bounties.

Acceptable Risks

Cursory research suggests that this is most likely to be an issue when you scan multiple hosts, or the same hosts multiple times, in rapid succession & send potentially disruptive traffic.

A couple Nmap scans done occasionally will probably be fine.

Safety Measures

Decreasing Traffic

Enumeration that generates less traffic will be less likely to be flagged by an ISP (or your target's defenses). For example, an Nmap SYN Stealth scan may be better than the default TCP Connect scan.

Save you scan results to a file, so you won't need to repeat scans as much (unless you want to double check the scan results, etc).

Decreasing Speed

Faster scans are more likely to be flagged, as there's a higher chance they will cause disruptions. The default speed for Nmap is probably fine (-T3), but ideally you'd reduce the scan speed speed further.

Obscuring your Identity

Ideally you'd conduct your Pentest using another host, so your ISP wouldn't tie it to your network. Depending on the type of enumeration done, options include using:

  1. A Cloud Server

  1. A Proxy (i.e. Nmap's Zombie Scan)

  1. Tor

Caveats: These will typically decrease your speed considerably. Depending on the type of enumeration, you may also encounter issues depending on the protocols you'll be using.
